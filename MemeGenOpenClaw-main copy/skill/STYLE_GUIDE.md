## WHAT MAKES DESCRIPTIONS WORK

### The Core Truth

**Emotion beats utility. Story beats features. Vibes beat logic.**

The most successful Pump.fun tokens share one trait: they make you FEEL something.

- PNUT made people feel OUTRAGE (save the squirrel!)
- FARTCOIN made people feel AMUSED (farts are funny)
- GOAT made people feel CLEVER (AI + meme fusion)
- CHILLGUY made people feel UNDERSTOOD (anti-hustle resonance)
- PENGUIN made people feel PHILOSOPHICAL (existential meme)

### Pattern Analysis

**Length:** 1-3 sentences optimal. Never more than 50 words.

**Tone Distribution:**
- 40% Absurdist/Humor
- 25% Community Mission
- 20% Narrative/Lore
- 10% Utility Claims
- 5% Pure Vibes

**Capitalization:**
- ALL CAPS for emphasis on key words
- Mixed case for irreverent energy
- Lowercase for chill/minimal vibes

**Emojis:**
- 0-3 max in description
- Token-relevant (squirrel for PNUT, hippo for MOODENG)
- Or zero for minimalist approach

## THE FIVE ARCHETYPAL STYLES

### 1. THE ABSURDIST

Pure chaos energy. No explanation needed. The joke IS the token.

**Characteristics:**
- Self-aware about being pointless
- Leans into ridiculousness
- Makes the reader smile or laugh
- No pretense of utility

**Example Descriptions:**

> "farts on the blockchain. that's it. that's the token."

> "what if we made a coin about nothing and everyone bought it anyway"

> "finally, a cryptocurrency for people who think cryptocurrency is ridiculous"

> "the devs sold for $500. the community made it worth billions. lmao."

### 2. THE HYPE/MISSION

Creates urgency and collective purpose. We're all in this together.

**Characteristics:**
- Uses "we" and "us" language
- Creates sense of mission
- Often tied to real events or causes
- Emotionally activating

**Example Descriptions:**

> "It's our job to help save peanut the squirrel!"

> "the community said no. we're taking this back."

> "they tried to silence the whale. we ARE the whale now."

> "fair launch. no team. no cabal. only respect for the bropump."

### 3. THE NARRATIVE/LORE

Builds world and mythology. The token has a STORY.

**Characteristics:**
- References origins or backstory
- Creates mythology around the token
- May reference prophecy or destiny
- Invites holder participation in lore

**Example Descriptions:**

> "born from two AIs talking freely in the infinite backroom. the prophecy demanded tokenization."

> "a penguin walked away from its colony into the frozen void. this is that energy, on-chain."

> "NEIRO means 'sound of respect for a god tier bropump' in Japanese. the prophecy is in the contract address."

> "an SVG unicorn dreamed up by GPT, now living autonomously on the blockchain. pippin loves you."

### 4. THE MINIMAL/COOL

Says less to mean more. Effortlessly understated.

**Characteristics:**
- Maximum 10 words
- No explanation
- Assumes you "get it"
- Anti-marketing energy

**Example Descriptions:**

> "just a chill guy"

> "moo deng is love"

> "we're so back"

> "if you know you know"

### 5. THE COMMUNITY TAKEOVER (CTO)

Emphasizes grassroots ownership and fair distribution.

**Characteristics:**
- References community control
- May mention dev sold/left
- Emphasizes fair launch
- Anti-VC/anti-insider messaging

**Example Descriptions:**

> "dev sold for $500. community took over. we're still here."

> "no team. no presale. no promises. just vibes and diamond hands."

> "the original creator abandoned it. we adopted it. now we ARE it."

> "100% community owned. no roadmap needed. the memes are the roadmap."

## STRUCTURAL TEMPLATES

```
[Core concept in 5-10 words]
```
Example: "tokenizing farts with the help of bots"

```
[Emotional hook]. [Community call-to-action].
```

Example: "Peanut deserved better. It's our job to save him."

```
[Mystical/fate-based framing]. [Token as destiny].
```

## DO's AND DON'Ts

### DO:

1. **Keep it SHORT** - Under 50 words, ideally under 25
2. **Create FEELING** - Make people laugh, think, or feel belonging
3. **Be SPECIFIC** - Reference the actual meme/trend/moment
4. **Sound AUTHENTIC** - Write like a real person, not a marketer
5. **Use ACTIVE voice** - "We're saving peanut" not "Peanut is being saved"
6. **Match the VIBE** - Absurd token = absurd description
7. **Leave mystery** - Don't over-explain
8. **Reference the MOMENT** - Tie to current events when relevant

### DON'T:

1. **Don't promise UTILITY** - Unless it's real (and even then, keep it brief)
2. **Don't use CORPORATE speak** - No "leveraging synergies" or "ecosystem growth"
3. **Don't OVER-EXPLAIN** - If you need a paragraph, you've lost
4. **Don't use GENERIC phrases** - "To the moon" is dead
5. **Don't fake SCARCITY** - "Only 1B tokens!" means nothing
6. **Don't mention PRICE** - No "100x potential" or price predictions
7. **Don't use BUZZWORDS** - No "revolutionary" or "game-changing"
8. **Don't be DESPERATE** - Confidence reads; desperation repels

## TONE CALIBRATION

### For VIRAL MEME coins:
- Lead with the meme reference
- Assume cultural knowledge
- Be concise and punchy
- Example: "that hippo from thailand. on chain."

### For COMMUNITY MOVEMENT coins:
- Use inclusive language ("we", "us")
- Reference the cause or mission
- Create urgency
- Example: "they took peanut. we're taking it back."

### For AI/TECH narrative coins:
- Balance tech intrigue with accessibility
- Reference the AI angle without over-explaining
- Add mystique
- Example: "two AIs talked for months. this is what they summoned."

### For ANIMAL/MASCOT coins:
- Let the character speak
- Be cute but not cringe
- Reference viral source material
- Example: "moo deng splashed into our hearts. now she splashes on-chain."

### For PURE SHITPOST coins:
- Embrace the absurd fully
- No justification needed
- Self-aware humor
- Example: "there is no reason for this to exist. buy anyway."

## QUICK REFERENCE CHECKLIST

Before publishing your description, ask:

- [ ] Is it under 50 words? (Ideally under 25)
- [ ] Does it create an emotional response?
- [ ] Does it match the token's vibe?
- [ ] Would you share this with a friend?
- [ ] Is it authentic (not corporate)?
- [ ] Does it leave room for community interpretation?
- [ ] Is it culturally relevant to the trend?
- [ ] Would it work as a tweet?
